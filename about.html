About Me



Hi, I'm Mengyu Liang, or Amber. I'm currently a senior studying Physical Geography, and Cartography/GIS at UW-Madison. This is my final semster in Madison and I have been looking forward to going off to explore other parts of the world after graduation in May.



My hometown is Kunming, a city in Southwestern China. Kunming is famous for its natural and cultural attractions. Growing up in Kunming makes me appreciate the beauty of nature and got me interested in learning about the natural landscape of different part of the world. Therefore, I decided to study abroad in the U.S during second year in high school. I have been in the U.S for six years now. I really appreciate the experience of being in a different country and being exposed to a completely different culture.



My current plan for the summer right after graduation is to do a summer internship in Japan. Ideally, I hope to work on a resarch project at a university or research institute. My reserach passions relate to adopting remote sensing techniques to study the Polar regions as well as volcanic activites. Lately I have been intrigued by using the cutting-edge VIIRS Day/Night band(DNB) on Suomi NPP to analyze effusive volcanic activities.
